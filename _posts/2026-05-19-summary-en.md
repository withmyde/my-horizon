## [FBI Seeks Nationwide License Plate Data](https://www.404media.co/the-fbi-wants-to-buy-nationwide-access-to-license-plate-readers/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The FBI has issued a request for information (RFI) seeking to purchase nationwide access to automated license plate reader (ALPR) data, enabling surveillance of vehicle movements across the country. This expansion of surveillance powers could fundamentally alter privacy expectations for all drivers, as law enforcement would gain the ability to track any vehicle's movements over time without warrants. It raises significant constitutional and civil liberties concerns. The RFI specifies that the FBI wants access to a 'national repository' of ALPR data with at least 5 billion plate reads, including historical data, and with near-real-time query capabilities. The data would come from private companies that operate ALPR networks.

hackernews · cdrnsf · May 18, 19:28 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48184350)

**Background**: Automated license plate readers (ALPRs) are camera systems that capture and store images of license plates, often used by law enforcement and toll collection. Private companies aggregate this data from various sources and sell access. The FBI's request marks a move towards a centralized, nationwide surveillance system that could be used for mass tracking.

## [SpaceX Starship V3 First Flight Crucial for IPO](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/spacex-needs-starship-v3-launch-to-deliver-ahead-of-planned-ipo) ⭐️ 8.0/10

SpaceX is targeting May 20, 2026 for the first test flight of its Starship V3 megarocket, which is a critical milestone to validate the rocket's capabilities ahead of a planned IPO in June. A successful Starship V3 flight would demonstrate SpaceX's next-generation launch capability, potentially boosting investor confidence for the IPO. It could also accelerate progress toward heavy-lift missions and satellite deployment. Starship V3 features Booster 19, Ship 39, and Raptor 3 engines, with a target capacity of over 100 metric tons to low Earth orbit. The launch will use the new Pad 2 at Starbase in Texas.
